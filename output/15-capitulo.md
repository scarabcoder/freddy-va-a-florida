# Capítulo XV

Por fin, tarde una noche, llegaron al fondo del oscuro bosque de pinos where they had discovered the empty cabaña de troncos, and where they had buried the oro. Aunque la luna brillaba con fuerza, el bosque estaba muy lúgubre, y avanzaban lentamente sin hablar mucho, pensando en cómo llevarían el oro back in the carriage, and how glad Mr. Bean would be when he saw it. Casi habían llegado a la cabaña when Robert and Jack both stopped at exactly the same moment and started sniffing the air.

"Huelo tabaco[^tabaco]," dijo Jack. "Y no es de los buenos."

"Viene de la dirección de la cabaña," dijo Robert. "Alguien está fumando."

"La gente honesta ya está acostada a estas horas," dijo Mrs. Wiggins. "Sea quien sea, no está tramando nada bueno. Hank y yo nos quedamos aquí, y los demás pueden acercarse sigilosamente to see what those people are up to."

So Freddy and Robert and Jack and Jinx went very quietly up to the cabaña. As soon as they got near it, vieron que había luz en la ventana.

"No me gusta nada esto," dijo Jinx. "Espero que no hayan encontrado nuestro oro."

"Ojalá que las golondrinas estuvieran despiertas," dijo Freddy. "Podríamos preguntarles. Pero vamos a asomarnos por la ventana."

Se acercaron sigilosamente y miraron, and there were three men sitting round the table smoking clay pipes. Eran hombres de aspecto muy rudo, with caps pulled down over their eyes, y todos llevaban revólveres[^revolveres] and dark linternas, so the animales knew at once that they were ladrones. On the table was a big heap of everything you can imagine — relojes de oro, carteras, dinero, tenedores de plata, aretes, brazaletes y joyas[^joyas] de todo tipo — all the things the burglars had stolen from the farmers who lived near the pine woods.

El más grande de los tres ladrones was dividing the heap into three parts. "Uno para ti, y uno para ti, y uno para mí. Uno para ti, y uno para ti, y uno para mí." Pero no lo estaba haciendo de manera justa. Cada vez que decía "Uno para ti," tomaba algo pequeño que no valía mucho — a small spoon or a ten-cent piece — and put it in front of one of his companions. Pero cuando decía "Uno para mí," agarraba un reloj de oro o un billete de diez dólares or a jewelled bracelet and put it in front of himself.

Pero los otros dos ladrones eran hombres mucho más pequeños y no se atrevían a decir nada, though they looked very discontented with their shares.

Ahora bien, la ventana through which the animales were looking was rather high up, and although the two perros and Freddy could see in by putting their forepaws on the sill and stretching their necks, Jinx was too short, and had to climb up and hang on by his garras. He didn't mind this, because his garras were sharp and strong. But there was a big brown polilla[^polilla] who was also trying to look in the ventana, and it kept fluttering right in front of Jinx's nose so that half the time he couldn't see a thing.

Al principio le habló con cortesía, y le pidió que subiera un poquito más arriba.

"¡Muévete tú!" gruñó la polilla. "Yo llegué primero."

"Claro que sí," dijo Jinx patiently. "Pero comprende que yo no puedo subir más. Y pensaba que la simple cortesía——"

"¡Cállate!" dijo la polilla.

So Jinx didn't say any more, but he made up his mind to teach that polilla a lesson. He let go with one paw, made one slap at the polilla, and knocked it right off the ventana.

But Robert, who was standing next to Jinx, was doing something that all dogs and a good many people do. When anything surprised him very much, abría los ojos de par en par, and his mouth seemed to come open too. So he was standing with his mouth wide open staring at the ladrones, and when Jinx hit the polilla, he knocked it straight down Robert's throat.

"¡Arrrrgh!" dijo Robert. "¡Guau!"

"¿Qué fue eso?" dijeron todos los ladrones at once, y se levantaron de un salto and bent over the table to blow out the lamp. Pero como los tres se inclinaron at exactly the same moment, sus tres cabezas chocaron justo en el centro — ¡crack! Y entonces la luz se apagó, and the animales couldn't see anything more, but they could hear the ladrones rubbing their bumped heads and groaning.

Los animales esperaron largo rato, pero no pasó nada. Los ladrones were clearly very frightened. Parecía que estaban cuchicheando, y al fin Jinx dijo: "Voy a entrar para ver qué están haciendo. Noté que la puerta estaba entreabierta, y creo que puedo colarme."

So he went around to the door, and sure enough it was open a crack, and he made himself narrow as cats can, and slipped in. Estaba tan oscuro adentro that the burglars could not see anything at all, but Jinx could see them quite plainly — cats can see in the dark. Se subió a la repisa[^repisa] de la chimenea to be out of the way, and sat down.

Los dos ladrones pequeños, cuyo nombres eran Ed y Bill, estaban en un rincón tratando de abrir una de las linternas, pero como nunca las usaban — las llevaban solo para parecer ladrones — no sabían cómo abrirla. El ladrón grande, whose name was Percy, estaba junto a la mesa, feeling with his fingers in the other piles and putting the biggest things onto his own pile. But he couldn't see what he was doing, and pretty soon he knocked a watch and an emerald necklace off onto the suelo.

Al oírlo, Ed y Bill se sobresaltaron. "¿Qué estás haciendo por ahí, Percy?" susurró Bill.

"¡Está echando mano a las joyas!" dijo Ed.

"¡Para nada!" dijo Percy. "Solo buscaba los cerillos."

"¿Ah sí?" dijo Ed. "Pues ven acá y ayúdanos con esta linterna."

So Percy hastily stuffed a handful of ten-dollar bills into his pocket and came over.

"¿Por qué no encienden el quinqué?" preguntó. "Estamos perfectamente seguros. Ese ruido no fue nada."

"Puede ser," dijo Bill. "Pero primero voy a echar un vistazo con la linterna."

Estaban todos muy juntos cerca de la chimenea, and as Percy took the lantern, Jinx — who could never resist a joke — reached out and dug his garras into Percy's shoulder.

"¡Ay!" gritó Percy, dropping the lantern with a crash. "¡¿Qué es eso de clavarme alfileres?!" And he struck out with his fist in the darkness and hit Bill on the nose.

Bill había ido a decir: "¡Yo no te toqué, tonto!" but when that hard fist hit him, changed his mind and flew at Percy, and in a second they were rolling on the floor and clawing and kicking and pulling each other's hair.

Se revolcaron hacia la mesa, and Ed, who was afraid they would knock it over and spill all the money and jewelry, took a cerillo from his pocket and scratched it on the repisa just under where Jinx was sitting, doubled up with laughter at the commotion he had caused. El cerillo flameó, and by its light Ed saw Jinx.

Now, if you are a rather timid ladrón, and you light a match in a dark room and see a gato that is half black and half red — for Jinx had been dipped in the paint pot, you remember — si ves semejante gato sonriéndote a un centímetro de tu nariz, probably you will do exactly what Ed did. Soltó el cerillo y lanzó un grito espantoso.

When he yelled, Bill and Percy stopped fighting and sat up. "¿Qué pasó?" preguntaron.

"¡Hay un gato rojo y negro sentado en la repisa de la chimenea sonriéndome!" dijo Ed with a scared voice.

"¡Tonterías!" dijo Percy, and Bill said: "¡Qué bobada!" and then he too lit a cerillo. He was near the ventana as he did so, and there was Freddy, the puerco, with his nariz against the glass, staring in for all he was worth.

Entonces fue el turno de Bill de soltar el cerillo y gritar. "¡Un puerco con lentes nos está mirando por la ventana!" For, of course, Freddy still had the circles around his eyes that Jinx had painted.

"¡Tonterías!" repitió Percy, but not quite as loudly. Y Bill y Ed no dijeron nada.

Hubo silencio por unos minutos, while the three scared ladrones tried to get up enough courage to light another cerillo. Entonces, en el silencio, llegó el débil sonido de ruedas en el camino de afuera.

"¡Escuchen!" susurró Percy. "Alguien viene. Voy a salir a ver. Whatever it is, it won't be black and red cats and pigs with spectacles, anyway." Y salió sigilosamente por la puerta.

Los otros dos ladrones se asomaron tímidamente a la puerta, but although it was bright moonlight, the trees were so thick round the cabaña that they couldn't see the camino. And then, as they waited, llegó un grito terrible — three times as loud as Ed's yell and Bill's yell put together. Oyeron pasos[^pasos] corriendo, and Percy dashed back to the door, his eyes nearly popping from his head with fright.

"¡Corran! ¡Corran por su vida!" jadeó. "¡En el camino hay un tigre[^tigre] uncido a un carruaje, y detrás del carruaje hay un leopardo con cuernos, tan grande como una vaca! ¡Corran, o nos comerán a todos!" And he dashed off into the woods and the two others rushed out after him, and the animales could hear the crash of branches and the thump of heavy feet die away in the distance. Y debo decir aquí que nunca volvieron a ver a Ed, a Bill, ni a Percy.

Claro que lo que había pasado era esto. Mrs. Wiggins and Hank had gotten tired of waiting, and when they had heard the first two yells they had started down the camino to see what was happening. No habían visto a Percy salir por la puerta, and when he saw them and let out his terrible yell, they had been even more frightened than he was. En efecto, Mrs. Wiggins se sintió tan mal that she had to lie down for a few minutes by the roadside while Charles and Henrietta fanned her with their alas.

"¡Se me fue el alma al suelo!" dijo. "¡Ay, ay, ay! Hank, pon una pata en mi costado y siente cómo me late el corazón. ¡Qué experiencia tan espantosa!"

But pretty soon she was able to get up and be helped inside the cabaña.

Los ladrones had left all the stolen things behind them in their flight, but as the animales had no cerillos, and as it was late, they decided not to do anything about them until morning. Y así todos se acurrucaron cómodamente en el suelo and went to sleep.
