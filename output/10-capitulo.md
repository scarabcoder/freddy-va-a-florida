# Capítulo X

Mientras seguían viajando, el tiempo se volvía más y más caluroso, so they got up very early in the mornings and did most of their travelling while it was still cool. About eleven o'clock they would stop under the sombra[^sombra] of a big tree by the roadside, and lie about in the zacate and talk until late in the tarde. And then they would go on for a while until they found a good camping-place. When they came to a río or an estanque, they would all go in swimming. Era la vida más agradable que you can imagine.

Un día, al mediodía, they were all sitting in the sombra beside the camino at the top of a steep colina. On the other side of the camino was a house, but nobody was in sight but a little niña[^nina], who was wheeling her muñecas[^munecas] up and down in a cochecito de muñecas[^cochecito].

Most of the animales were asleep, because Jinx, the gato, had been talking, and nobody paid any attention when he talked. That didn't make any difference to Jinx, though. He went right on telling how smart he was and bragging about what he could do.

That was the worst of Jinx: siempre hablaba de sí mismo. If the animales talked about automobiles, he told how much _he_ knew about them; and if they dijo: "Let's go in swimming," he told what a fine swimmer _he_ was, although they all knew he hated the agua and couldn't swim two strokes.

Hoy estaba hablando de bicicletas[^bicicleta].

"'Tisn't anything to ride a bicicleta," dijo. "I've ridden 'em—all kinds—bicycles and tricycles and velocipedes and——"

"¡Eres un fanfarrón![^fanfarron]" dijo Freddy crossly, and all the other animales who were awake dijo: "¡Por favor, cállate, Jinx!"

Alice and Emma, the two white patos, didn't say anything, however, because they were always very polite. They were almost too polite, if such a thing is possible. But they were just as tired of hearing Jinx talk as the others were; so Alice dijo: "Come on, Emma; let's go play with the niña." And they got up and ruffled out their plumas and waddled sedately across the camino and up the path to the house.

La niña was delighted to have someone to play with, and she put the patos in the cochecito de muñecas with the two muñecas and pretended that they were the neighbour's children, and that she had to look after them while their mother was out shopping. She pretended that they might catch cold and wrapped them up in a little cobija, and Alice and Emma were so polite that they let her do it, although it was so hot that they nearly boiled.

Then la niña dijo: "Are you comfortable, darlings?"

And Emma dijo: "Quack, quack!"

"¡Oh!" dijo la niña. "She can say '¡Mamá!'" And Emma had to keep on quacking for quite a long time while la niña hopped up and down and clapped her hands.

By and by la niña got tired of this and dijo she would take them for a ride, so she wheeled them down the path and out into the camino. Then she saw a bright blue mariposa[^mariposa] and ran off across the field after it, leaving the cochecito standing in the camino at the top of the colina, near where the animales were resting.

Jinx was still talking about bicicletas.

"I can ride backwards, and with both paws off the handle bars, and I can ride up and down stairs——"

"¡Deja de decir tonterías!" dijo Henrietta. "You couldn't ride a bicicleta. Your legs aren't long enough to reach the pedals."

"They wouldn't have to be," dijo Jinx. "I could do all that going down colina. Just start at the top, and _whizzz!_—down you go at sixty miles an hour! And——"

"¡Basta ya!" exclamó Henrietta. "¡Nunca he escuchado a un animal así! ¡Fanfarrón! You wouldn't dare ride down that colina in that cochecito de muñecas there!"

"Ho!" dijo Jinx. "That's nothing! That's so easy it isn't worth bothering about."

"All right," dijo Henrietta. "Let's see you do it, then."

"I suppose you think I can't?" dijo Jinx.

"I think you won't," dijo Henrietta bluntly.

Jinx se levantó and walked over to the cochecito and climbed into it beside Alice and Emma and the two muñecas.

"Why, it isn't anything," dijo. "It isn't anything at _all_! Just slide down that colina? Pooh!" But he didn't seem very anxious to start.

"Please get out of the cochecito, Jinx," dijo Emma. "There isn't room for all of us in here."

"Are you really going to slide down the colina, Jinx?" preguntó Alice. "Because if you are, I'm going to get out."

"¿Bajar esa colina?" dijo Jinx. "And climb all the way back up again in the hot sol, just to prove I can do it? Huh! I should say not! If they don't believe me—well, they needn't, that's all!"

All the animales had waked up by now and had come out into the camino.

"You don't dare slide down the colina," they shouted. "¡Cobarde![^cobarde] ¡Miedoso!" And Freddy made up a verse and sang it while he danced around the cochecito on his hind patas.

>              _"'Fraid cat Jinx,_
>               _His tail's full of kinks!_
>                 _He doesn't dare slide down the hill!_
>               _See how he shrinks!"_

Now Jinx had no intention of sliding down the colina, which was a good milla long, with a curva[^curva] at the bottom, and he was thinking hard for some good excuse. But while he was hesitating, Freddy bumped against the wheel of the cochecito and gave it just enough of a push to start it slowly down the colina.

"¡Oye! ¿Qué estás haciendo?" yelled Jinx, too frightened to jump.

Los animales dejaron de gritar and stood with their mouths open as the cochecito de muñecas gathered speed and shot away from them down the steep colina. They heard the scared quacking of Alice and Emma, and saw their little white cabezas peering fearfully out; they saw Jinx holding on for dear life with all his twenty garras as the cochecito jumped and bounded from side to side of the camino. And then it grew smaller and smaller and disappeared round the curva.

Los animales estaban muy asustados, and they started down the colina as fast as they could go. Half-way down they heard a great noise behind them, and it was la niña, who was coming after them, crying and sobbing at the loss of her muñecas.

"¡Ese gato malo!" she wailed. "¡Ese gato malo y perverso! He stole my cochecito and ran off with my dollies!"

Los animales esperaron until she caught up, and Hank knelt down and let her climb up on his back. Then they went on.

Pretty soon they got to the curva at the foot of the colina. They went round it, and there was a puente crossing a wide stream, and half-way across the puente lay the cochecito, upside down, and a very wet Jinx, with a moretón[^moreton] over one ojo, was crawling up on to the bank out of the agua. And out in the middle of the stream Alice and Emma were swimming about and quacking as if nothing had happened.

When the cochecito had turned over, it had been going so fast that the patos and the muñecas and the gato had been thrown way up over the top of the puente into the agua. The muñecas had sunk, and the gato had sunk too, for a few minutes and had had a hard time getting ashore, for he wasn't much of a swimmer in spite of his bragging. But Alice and Emma hadn't minded a bit.

As soon as Jinx saw his amigos, he tried to look as if he had done it on purpose.

"There!" dijo. "I guess you won't dare _me_ to do anything again! I guess I did it, didn't I? I guess _you_ haven't got much to say!"

But la niña jumped down from Hank's back and went over to him and began slapping him good and hard.

"¡Gato malo!" she cried. "¡Gato malo, perverso! ¿Dónde están mis muñecas?"

Jinx made himself as small as possible and put his head down between his patas and let her spank him. It didn't hurt as much as she thought it did, and as he dijo afterwards to Freddy—"it knocked all the agua out of my fur."

But Alice and Emma dove for the muñecas and brought them up and laid them on the orilla to dry. And after a while, when la niña was tired of spanking Jinx, she put them into the cochecito again and Mrs. Wiggins pushed it back up the colina for her. But la niña rode up on Hank's back.

After that, Jinx no hablaba tanto. And if he did begin to boast, all the animales had to do was to say: "¡Ladrón de muñecas! ¡Fanfarrón! ¿Quién fue al que una niña azotó?" And he would curl up and pretend to go to sleep.
