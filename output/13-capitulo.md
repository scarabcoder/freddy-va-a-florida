# Capítulo XIII

Después de la aventura con los caimanes the animals rested for dos días, and then they went on seeing the sights of Florida. Hicieron muchos amigos agradables among the native animals, and even el señor Webb and la señora Webb made the acquaintance of several very interesting arañas, with whom they discussed fly-catching and compared notes on weaving and other matters.

But at last one morning when they awoke, el cielo estaba lleno de bandadas de pájaros — bluebirds and blackbirds and red-wings and yellow-hammers — all flying steadily north. Y entonces supieron que la primavera había llegado and it was time to start back home.

"Bueno, I for one shall be glad to get back," dijo Mrs. Wiggins. "We've had a wonderful time, pero home is a pretty good place. La nieve ya se habrá ido, and Mr. Bean is getting ready to plant his potatoes and corn."

"Y el viejo olmo junto al granero estará lleno de brotes nuevos," dijo Charles.

"And the ice is gone out of the duck pond," dijo Alice and Emma together.

"Y Mr. Bean me necesitará para la labranza de primavera," dijo Hank.

"¡Ándale pues, animales!" dijo Freddy. "¡Vámonos!" Y así se despidieron de Florida and started for home.

Llevaban viajando about a week when one morning they came to a big field all heaped with tin cans and old shoes and ashes and rubbish of every kind. Era un basurero[^basurero]. Había cardos[^cardos] espinosos growing in the field, and a chivo[^chivo] was eating them.

"Buenos días, chivo," dijo Freddy.

"Buenos días, puerco," dijo el chivo. "¿Quieres un cardo? Son deliciosos."

"No, gracias," dijo Freddy.

"¿Alguna vez has comido uno?" preguntó el chivo.

"No," dijo Freddy. "Nunca me han parecido muy apetecibles."

"Te sorprenderías," dijo el chivo, "de lo sabrosos que son. Prueba nada más un mordisco de éste grande."

Freddy didn't want to try the cardo, but he was always very polite and didn't like to hurt the chivo's feelings, so tomó un gran mordisco.

¡Qué error tan grande cometió! The prickles tickled his mouth horribly and stuck in his tongue, and he coughed and sneezed and squealed and ran round and round in circles, mientras los otros animales se reían y el chivo lo miraba con sorpresa. Y al fin logró escupirlo.

"Lo siento mucho," dijo el chivo. "Perhaps there was something wrong with that one. Aquí hay otro muy bueno. Or perhaps you'd rather have a bit of old boot. I ate one already, but——"

"No, gracias," dijo Freddy firmly. "Some people say a pig will eat anything, pero uno tiene que tener ciertos límites. And I draw the line at old boots."

"Bueno, bueno," dijo el chivo with a sigh, "de gustos no hay nada escrito. I had hoped to persuade you animals to settle down and live here with me. Pero si no les gustan los cardos ni las botas——"

"No nos gustan," dijo Mrs. Wiggins. "A ninguno de nosotros."

"Entonces ya no hay más que hablar," dijo el chivo sorrowfully. "Because there's really nothing else here. A mí me gusta. Pero es muy solitario. No tengo a nadie con quien hablar all day but the stupid cart-horses who bring the rubbish here to be dumped. Y me encanta la buena conversación."

Estaba tan solo que los animales stayed the rest of the day with him and told him all about their viaje. Just as they were leaving, late in the tarde, a farm wagon came along piled high with cosas viejas. El hombre que conducía was moving into another house and had brought everything he didn't want. Tiró toda la carga[^carga] del vagón and drove away. On the very top was a funny old-fashioned carruaje.

"Must be some boots in that lot," dijo el chivo, licking his lips, y empezó a rebuscar entre el montón.

But Jinx and Freddy had walked over to look at the old carruaje. Hablaron en voz baja for a few minutes, and then Jinx dijo: "¡Oye, Hank! ¿Crees que podrías jalar este carruaje?"

"¿Jalar _ése_?" dijo Hank indignantly. "I've drawn heavier wagons than that many a time."

"Oh, I know you can draw it," dijo Jinx. "Lo que quiero decir es — ¿puedes jalarlo como está, without any arnés[^arnes] or straps or things?"

"No," dijo Hank. "I'd have to have a collar and traces and bridle and bit and surcingle and——"

"Oh, none of us know what any of those things are," dijo Jinx, "and anyway we haven't got them. Pero aquí hay un pedazo de cuerda vieja. ¿Y si la atamos a las manijas del carruaje and put it over your shoulders? ¿Podrías jalarlo así?"

"Pues, no digo que no pudiera," dijo Hank. "But what do you want the carriage for anyway?"

"Si puedes jalarlo," dijo Jinx, "podemos poner todo el oro que encontramos en la casa de las hormigas inside it and take it back to Mr. Bean."

A Hank le pareció un plan excelente, so Freddy got the cuerda and Jinx tied it to the handles. All gatos are good at tying knots — the stupidest cat can tie forty knots in a ball of yarn in two minutes. So this was easy for Jinx. Y así pusieron la cuerda sobre los hombros de Hank, and he pulled the carruaje up onto the camino.

El carruaje tenía dos asientos and the top was like a square umbrella with flecos[^flecos] hanging from the edges. Se llamaba faetón[^faeton], and if you think that a funny name, all I can say is that it was a very funny carruaje indeed. Los animales se rieron a carcajadas when they saw Hank pulling it out of the basurero, and Mrs. Wiggins laughed so hard she had to lie down right in the middle of all the old tin cans.

But when Freddy les explicó para qué podían usarlo, and how they could carry the oro back to Mr. Bean, todos estaban muy contentos. Los dos perros gathered some things they thought Mr. Bean would like and put them in the faetón — an old straw sombrero, an old overcoat, and two buckets, one half full of red paint and one half full of green paint. "Can use those to paint the house," dijo Robert. Y también encontraron a plaid shawl for Mrs. Bean. These were things people had thrown away at the basurero, but the animales thought Mr. Bean could use them.

Entonces se despidieron del chivo. He didn't feel so bad about their going now, because he had a fresh wagon-load of cosas to look over, and had already found many more old boots to chew on. Then all the small animales climbed into the faetón and they started off. Robert and Emma and Jack sat on the front seat; Freddy and Alice and Jinx and Henrietta on the back seat. Los cuatro ratones played tag all over the carruaje for a while, and then they curled up in the bottom and went to sleep. Y Charles se posó on top of the square umbrella.

When they had gone a little way, they looked back and waved good-bye to the chivo, and he waved back at them. Podían verlo chewing away contentedly, and the ends of two old shoe-strings were hanging out of his mouth.

[^basurero]: basurero (rubbish dump)
[^cardos]: cardos (thistles)
[^chivo]: chivo (goat)
[^carga]: carga (load / cargo)
[^arnes]: arnés (harness)
[^flecos]: flecos (fringe)
[^faeton]: faetón (phaeton — old open carriage)
