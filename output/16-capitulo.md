# Capítulo XVI

Alice no durmió muy bien esa noche. Tenía un dolor de estómago[^dolordestomago]. Y tenía dolor de estómago because she had eaten two chocolates and a caramel and a horehound drop that Robert had given her from a bag of candy he had found by the roadside. Robert le había ofrecido a Emma también, but she had very sensibly refused it. Los dulces no le caen bien a los patos.

So, as she couldn't sleep no matter how hard she tried, Alice se levantó antes del amanecer y salió al bosque. El fresco aire matutino la dio sueño, so she thought she would try again, and having found a sheltered spot under a big pine, she tucked her head under her wing and dozed off. When she woke up, el sol ya brillaba and the golondrinas were pouring like smoke out of the chimney in search of their breakfast.

Alice le llamó a una de ellas and asked about the burglars.

"Llevan aquí alrededor de un mes," dijo la golondrina. "Salen todas las noches to rob the farmers' houses, and then come back and sleep all day. Usually they get back about this time every morning, so you animals had better look out."

"No creo que vuelvan esta mañana," dijo Alice. "But tell me — ¿desenterraron el oro que encontramos cuando estuvimos aquí antes?"

"No," dijo la golondrina. "No lo han tocado."

"Gracias," dijo Alice. "Era lo único que quería saber. Buenos días." Y corrió de vuelta to tell the others that their treasure was safe.

But when she got back to the cabaña, se detuvo asombrada en la puerta. Her sister Emma waddled importantly up and down with a bracelet set with big blue sapphires round her neck and a beautiful purse, all made of little links of pure gold, tucked under her wing. Los cuatro ratones, with diamond rings round their necks like collars, were playing tag in a corner, and they sparkled and glittered like little streaks of fire as they chased one another. Henrietta looked very queenly with a hoop of rubíes[^rubies] set on her head like a crown. She was bending down trying to see herself in the little mirror set in the cover of a powder box, which she had snapped open with her claw. But Mrs. Wiggins was most gorgeous of all. Llevaba un collar[^collar] de perlas[^perlas] alrededor de su grande cuello, y un reloj de platino en su tobillo izquierdo. Se había colgado un collar de esmeraldas en cada cuerno, and they hung down and dangled beside her broad, pleasant face like enormous earrings. Y se había empolvado la nariz ancha y negra with polvos[^polvos] until it was as white as flour. She looked truly magnificent.

Alice, after a moment's astonishment, entered into the fun. She found a thin gold chain with a diamond locket which she hung round her neck, and then she went over to where Henrietta was still admiring herself in the powder-box cover, and asked if she might have some powder for her bill.

"Ya no queda," dijo Henrietta.

"Lo siento, Alice," dijo Mrs. Wiggins. "Me temo que lo usé todo. Hay tanto de mí que empolvar, ¿sabes? Ojalá pudiera verme. Though I must say I don't think I've improved my appearance much. Debo parecer una lavandera recargadísima de joyas. No hay mucho que puedas hacer con una vaca," añadió tristemente.

Entonces Robert y Jack jugaron a un juego. Each took six pulseras[^pulsera], and Mrs. Wiggins stood very still, and they tried to throw them over her cuernos. But they weren't very good at it, and after Mrs. Wiggins had been hit on the nose several times, dijo que prefería no seguir jugando, ya que le estaban quitando todos los polvos.

Entonces Henrietta dijo: "¿Qué vamos a hacer con todas estas joyas?"

"Deberíamos devolvérselas a la gente a quien se las robaron," dijo Hank.

"Muy bonito eso," dijo Henrietta. "¿Pero cómo propones hacerlo?"

Hank dijo que no sabía exactamente. So they talked it over for a while and at last hit upon a plan. Y después del desayuno cargaron todas las joyas en el faetón and started out for the nearest farm-house.

When they got there, no había nadie a la vista, but Jack and Robert barked until at last a woman came to the door to see what the matter was. Era una mujer grande y robusta, and she looked quite a lot like Mrs. Wiggins. Se estaba secando la espuma de jabón de las manos en el delantal, because she had been washing her husband's other shirt.

"¡Válgame el cielo!" exclamó when she saw the animales all grouped about the carriage. "¿Qué es esto, un circo?"

Le tomó bastante tiempo a los perros hacerle entender what they wanted her to do. Corrían de un lado a otro entre ella y el carruaje, and at last she followed them. When she saw the heap of money and jewelry, she gave a loud cry and seized the hoop of rubíes that Henrietta had worn on her head.

"¡Por el amor de Dios!" gritó. "¡Aquí está el anillo que me regaló la prima Eunice en Navidad, el que los ladrones se robaron cuando irrumpieron en nuestra casa el mes pasado! ¡Y aquí está el collar de esmeraldas que gané como premio en el club de cartas el invierno pasado! ¡Y aquí está la cigarrera de oro de Hiram!"

Corrió a la esquina de la casa. "¡Hiram! ¡Hiram!" gritó. "¡Ven aquí ahora mismo!"

So pretty soon Hiram, her husband, came from where he had been resting up in the hayloft. Y encontró veinte dólares, besides the cigarette case, that the burglars had taken from him.

"¿Cómo crees que estos animales consiguieron todo esto?" dijo. "¿Crees que encontraron el lugar donde los ladrones lo escondían?"

"No sé," dijo su esposa. "Pero lo que sí sé es que lo trajeron aquí para que pudiéramos recuperar lo nuestro. ¡Qué animales tan buenos y listos! ¡Los voy a besar a todos!" And she did, even the mice, who were scared to death. Se veía muy cómica después de besar a Mrs. Wiggins, because a lot of the powder had come off on her face.

"Ahora bien," dijo, "voy a llevar a estos animales a casa de la tía Etta, porque vi su sopera de oro entre esas cosas." Y se subió al faetón and they started off, while Hiram went back to do some more resting in the hayloft.

La tía Etta era una mujer instruida. Every evening she sat on the porch and read the newspaper until it got so dark she couldn't see, and then she went in and lit the lamp and finished reading it.

So when she had taken her gold soup tureen and one or two other things that the burglars had stolen, dijo: "Sé quiénes son estos animales. La semana pasada leí un artículo sobre ellos en el periódico. Están migrando. Vinieron del lejano norte y fueron a Florida para el invierno. Son animales muy listos de verdad. Supongo que ahora van camino a casa, ya que es primavera."

"Bueno," dijo su sobrina, "a ese paso no llegarán a casa hasta el otoño. Tendrían que visitar alrededor de cien granjas para devolver todo a sus dueños. Lástima que no haya una manera más rápida."

"Muchas de las cosas las pusieron como extraviadas en el periódico," dijo la tía Etta. "¿Qué tal si ponemos un anuncio[^anuncio] en el periódico diciendo que todas las cosas están aquí y que la gente puede venir a recogerlas? Así los animales no tendrían que recorrer todo el campo, and they could go on home in a day or two."

A la sobrina le pareció una buena idea, y los animales se miraron unos a otros y asintieron, and so Robert barked very loud to show that they thought it a good idea too. Entonces la tía Etta se levantó. "Voy a llamar a la oficina del periódico ahora mismo," dijo, "para que pongan el anuncio esta noche. Y luego les daremos algo de comer a estos animales and a place to be comfortable. Deben de estar cansados, having come such a long way."

So she telephoned the newspaper office, and then went out to the granero and got some oats for Hank, and she showed Alice and Emma where the estanque was and introduced them to her own ducks, and she found two bones for the perros, and a piece of cheese for the mice, and a saucer of cream for Jinx, and she cooked up some cornmeal mush for Charles and Henrietta, and led Mrs. Wiggins out to the pasture, where there was a very superior quality of zacate. If she had noticed el señor Webb and la señora Webb she would probably have tried to catch some flies for them, she was such a kind and generous old lady, and so grateful for the return of her gold soup tureen.

Luego, cuando todos los animales habían recibido lo que más les gustaba comer, she sat down on the porch and told her niece everything she had read in the newspaper for the last six weeks.
